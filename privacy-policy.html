Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 19, 2026 · Last updated: May 19, 2026

Voca-Voca ("we", "our", "Voca-Voca") is a vocabulary-learning service offered as a browser extension, a website at voca-voca.app, and a companion iOS app. The service lets you save words you encounter while reading, look up their definitions, and practice them with spaced repetition. This Privacy Policy explains what personal data we collect, why we collect it, how we store it, who we share it with, and what rights you have over it.

By installing or using Voca-Voca you agree to this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use and uninstall the application.

1. Data We Collect

We collect only the data needed to operate the service, deliver the features you signed up for, and improve the product over time. We do not sell personal data, we do not collect data for advertising, and we do not build profiles of users for behavioral targeting.

2. How We Use Your Data

  • Operating the service — looking up definitions, saving words, running practice sessions, displaying your progress, and synchronizing your collection across the devices you sign in on.
  • Managing your subscription — verifying that your Pro entitlement is active, processing renewals, restoring purchases on new devices.
  • Improving the product — analyzing anonymous interaction events to identify bugs, prioritize features, and measure the effect of changes we ship.
  • Security and compliance — detecting abuse, preventing fraud, complying with applicable law, and enforcing our Terms of Service.
  • Communicating with you — sending operational messages such as receipts, account-deletion confirmations, or critical security notices to the email address on file. We do not send marketing email unless you have explicitly opted in.

We do not use your data for advertising, third-party marketing, profiling, or any purpose unrelated to operating and improving Voca-Voca.

3. How We Store and Protect Your Data

Local storage

The browser extension and iOS app store your data on your device first. The browser extension uses Chrome's chrome.storage.local API; the iOS app uses SwiftData. Local data remains on your device for as long as the application is installed.

Cloud storage

When you sign in, your data syncs to Google Firebase Firestore. Firestore data is:

  • Encrypted in transit (TLS) and at rest (AES-256).
  • Stored under your unique Firebase user ID, isolated from all other users by Firestore Security Rules that enforce per-user read and write access.
  • Hosted on Google Cloud infrastructure. See Firebase Privacy and Security for Google's commitments.

Security measures

  • Firestore Security Rules prevent any user from accessing another user's data.
  • Authentication tokens are managed by Firebase Auth and are never stored in plain text.
  • We do not operate our own servers or databases. All cloud infrastructure is provided by Google Firebase and a small number of named third-party services listed in Section 4.
  • We review our security rules and access controls periodically.

5. Product Analytics

We use PostHog to collect anonymous interaction events. The purpose of this data is to operate and improve Voca-Voca: identifying which features people use, where they encounter friction, which release introduces a regression, and whether a change we shipped had the intended effect.

What is collected

  • Lifecycle events: install, update, session start, session end, uninstall.
  • Authentication events: that a sign-in occurred and which provider was used (Apple or Google). We do not collect the email address or name in analytics events.
  • Engagement events: word looked up, word saved, practice session started/completed, audio played, controller connected.
  • Funnel events: free-plan limit reached, upgrade clicked, upgrade succeeded.
  • Anonymous metadata about saved words: word length, language, part of speech, and source domain (for example, the bare hostname "nytimes.com"). We do not collect the word itself or the full URL.
  • Device context that PostHog records automatically: device type, operating system, browser (where applicable), language, and an approximate country or region inferred from IP address.

Events are keyed by your Firebase user ID once you sign in, and by a random anonymous identifier before sign-in. We do not link analytics data to your email address, real name, or any other personally identifying information.

Where analytics runs

  • The browser extension sends events from its background service worker and side panel directly to PostHog's HTTPS capture endpoint.
  • The iOS app uses the PostHog Swift SDK. Events stop being sent when you sign out.
  • The voca-voca.app marketing site uses the PostHog JavaScript SDK to count visits and measure which Chrome Web Store install links convert.

Your choices

  • You can sign out of the iOS app or the browser extension at any time; analytics events stop transmitting until you sign back in.
  • You can request deletion of your analytics data by emailing support@voca-voca.app. We will remove your events from PostHog within thirty (30) days.
  • Browser-level controls such as Brave Shields, uBlock Origin, or Privacy Badger will block PostHog requests from the marketing site. The extension and iOS app will continue to function normally regardless.

Analytics data is used solely by Voca-Voca to operate and improve the service. We do not sell it, license it, transfer it to data brokers, or combine it with information from third parties.

6. Subscriptions and Payments

Voca-Voca offers paid Pro subscriptions. Payment processing is handled entirely by third-party processors:

  • On iOS, purchases go through Apple StoreKit per Apple's App Store rules.
  • On the web, purchases go through Stripe.

In both cases, your full card details are collected and held by the processor — Voca-Voca never sees them. We receive only the information needed to know whether your subscription is active: the transaction identifier, the plan you bought, the renewal date, and whether the subscription is currently active, expired, or in a trial period. This information is mirrored to RevenueCat (Section 4) so your entitlement applies across every device you sign into.

9. Data Retention

  • Local data stays on your device for as long as the application is installed. Uninstalling removes all local data.
  • Cloud data is retained for as long as your account is active. You may delete individual words, your entire collection, or your account at any time (see Section 10).
  • Soft-deleted items (for example, a word you deleted) remain in our system for up to thirty (30) days for sync reconciliation, after which they are removed from all devices.
  • Analytics events are retained for up to twelve (12) months in PostHog before automatic deletion.
  • Inactive accounts: If you do not use Voca-Voca for twenty-four (24) months, we may delete your cloud data after sending a notification to your registered email address.
