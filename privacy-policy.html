Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 14, 2026 · Last updated: May 18, 2026

Voca-Voca ("we", "our", or "the extension") is a vocabulary-learning browser extension and companion iOS app that lets you look up word definitions while reading online, save words to a personal collection, and practice with spaced repetition. This Privacy Policy explains what data we collect, why we collect it, how we store and protect it, and what rights you have over it.

By installing or using Voca-Voca you agree to this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please uninstall the extension and discontinue use.

1. Data We Collect

We collect only what is necessary to provide the core vocabulary-learning features and to understand how Voca-Voca is used in aggregate. We do not collect data for advertising, profiling, or behavioral targeting. We use Google Analytics 4 for product analytics; see Section 5 for details and opt-out.

  • We do not read, collect, or store the content of web pages you visit.
  • We do not record your browsing history or navigation behavior.
  • We do not collect health, financial, or payment card information.
  • We do not use advertising cookies, third-party tracking pixels, or cross-site tracking technologies.   • We do not collect the actual words you save. Analytics events about saved words contain only anonymous metadata (word length, language, part of speech, source domain) — never the word itself.

2. How We Use Your Data

  • Providing core features — looking up definitions, saving words, running practice sessions, and displaying your progress.
  • Cloud sync — if you sign in with Google, syncing your vocabulary, progress, and settings across your devices.
  • Improving the product — we may use aggregated, non-identifiable usage patterns (e.g., average collection size) to improve Voca-Voca. Individual user data is never analyzed for this purpose.

We do not use your data for advertising, marketing, profiling, selling, or any purpose unrelated to the extension's vocabulary-learning functionality.

3. How We Store and Protect Your Data

Local storage

Your data is stored locally in your browser using Chrome's chrome.storage.local API. This data never leaves your device unless you sign in and enable cloud sync.

Cloud storage

If you sign in with Google, your data is synced to Google Firebase Firestore. Firestore data is:

  • Encrypted in transit (TLS) and at rest (AES-256).
  • Stored under your unique Firebase user ID, isolated from all other users by Firestore Security Rules that enforce per-user read/write access.
  • Hosted on Google Cloud infrastructure. For details see Firebase Privacy and Security.

Security measures

  • Firestore Security Rules prevent any user from accessing another user's data.
  • Authentication tokens are managed by Firebase Auth and are never stored in plain text.
  • We do not operate our own servers or databases — all cloud infrastructure is managed by Google Firebase.
  • We conduct periodic reviews of our security rules and access controls.

5. Analytics

Voca-Voca uses Google Analytics 4 ("GA4") to understand aggregate usage patterns — how many people install the extension, which features they use, where they drop off, and where they convert. We use this information to prioritize improvements and to demonstrate product traction to potential partners or investors.

Where analytics runs

  • This website (voca-voca.app) — loads the standard GA4 tag (gtag.js) on page view, gated by your consent choice (see "Opt-out" below).
  • Chrome extension — sends events to GA4 via the Measurement Protocol (server-to-server style HTTPS requests) from the extension's background service worker. No third-party JavaScript is loaded inside the extension; the Chrome Web Store's Manifest V3 rules prohibit that and we comply.
  • iOS app — once published, the iOS app will use Firebase Analytics (Google's iOS SDK that reports into the same GA4 property). This section will be effective for the iOS app once it launches.

Event categories we collect

We collect named events describing what the user did. The exact event payloads contain only the parameters listed below — never the word content you save, never the URL of the page you were reading, never your email or name.

  • Lifecycle events — install, update, session_start, extension_uninstalled. Counts of installs, updates, sessions, and uninstalls.
  • Authentication events — sign_in_succeeded with a method parameter (e.g., "google" or "apple"). Records that a sign-in occurred and which provider was used; does not include your email, name, or user ID.
  • Engagement events — word_lookup, word_saved, first_word_saved, milestone_10_words, practice_session_started, practice_session_completed, audio_played, gamepad_connected. Each event records that the action happened. The word_saved event additionally records anonymous metadata: word length (an integer), language (always "en"), part of speech (e.g., "noun"), and source domain (e.g., "nytimes.com") — but never the actual word.
  • Conversion events — free_limit_reached, upgrade_clicked, upgrade_succeeded. Tracks the funnel from hitting the free-plan limit to upgrading to Premium.
  • Outbound click events — cta_chrome_click on this website, with a link_location parameter (e.g., "nav", "hero", "final_cta") to measure which page placement drives Chrome Web Store installs.

What is sent to Google

Each event includes:

  • The event name and the parameters listed above.
  • An anonymous client ID: a random UUID generated on first use, stored locally on your device. The client ID is not linked to your name, email, or any other personal information. It exists so we can distinguish "1,000 visits by 1 person" from "1 visit each by 1,000 people."
  • Standard analytics metadata that Google receives automatically: device type, browser, language, approximate country/region (inferred from IP — see below), time of day.

We have enabled IP address anonymization in our GA4 configuration. Google receives only an anonymized IP and does not store it after deriving approximate region. We have not enabled Google Signals, Audience Manager, or any cross-product Google advertising integrations; analytics data is never shared with Google's advertising products.

Data retention is set to GA4's standard (currently 14 months) before automatic deletion at the user level.

Opt-out

Analytics is governed by Google Consent Mode v2:

  • The first time you visit voca-voca.app, all analytics consent flags default to denied — GA4 buffers events in memory but transmits nothing.
  • A small cookie banner asks whether you accept analytics cookies. The "Decline" button locks the consent flag to denied permanently for your browser. The "OK" button updates the flag to granted and only then does any analytics data leave the page.
  • Your choice is stored both in localStorage and a first-party cookie (so it survives if either storage mechanism is cleared by your browser's privacy controls). To change your decision later, clear your site data for voca-voca.app and the banner will reappear.
  • You can also use a browser-level opt-out such as Google's Google Analytics opt-out browser add-on, which blocks all GA4 requests at the network layer.

For the Chrome extension, analytics is currently always-on (gated only by the same Google Consent Mode defaults for first-party site analytics, which the extension respects). A user-visible toggle to disable extension analytics is planned for a future release; until then, you can disable analytics by uninstalling the extension.

Analytics data is used solely by Voca-Voca to improve the product. We do not sell it, share it with third parties for their own use, or combine it with personal information.

9. Data Retention

  • Cloud data is retained for as long as your account is active. You can delete individual words, your entire collection, or all cloud data at any time (see Section 10).
  • Soft-deleted items (words you delete) are marked as deleted and removed from all devices within 30 days.
  • If you do not use Voca-Voca for 24 months, we may delete your cloud data after sending a notification to your registered email address.
